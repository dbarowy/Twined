European Fascism

European Fascism refers to the political ideology and movement that emerged in Europe in the early 20th century, characterized by dictatorial power, extreme nationalism, and suppression of opposition. It had significant impacts in several countries, notably Germany, Italy, and Spain.

  • Germany: In Germany, fascism was led by Adolf Hitler, who established a totalitarian regime under the Nazi Party, promoting Aryan supremacy and leading to World War II.
  • Italy: In Italy, Benito Mussolini founded the Fascist Party, which emphasized aggressive nationalism and militarism, ultimately creating a dictatorial state.
  • Spain: In Spain, Francisco Franco led a fascist dictatorship following the Spanish Civil War, maintaining power through authoritarian rule until his death.
